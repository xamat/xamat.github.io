My Music

What I've done

Although I started studying music when I was quite little, I did not take it seriously until I was 15. Then I started to study piano at the Estudi school of modern music in Granollers. Although my level at the instrument never got very high, it was quite enough to enroll in my first band. It was a band created at the same school and we had a teacher in the rehearsals. With them I did my first concert. We played four songs (I don't remember all, but one was Allmand Brothers' and another one was a blues by Sting).

There were some interesting people in the audience and a few weeks later the Delbisness band was formed. With this band we only played covers. To name a few, I remember playing songs by: The Clash (Death or Glory), Rolling Stones (Dead Flowers), Ramones (Beat on the Brat), The Kinks (You Really Got Me), Burnin and Los Suaves. We did a few concerts that were quite "succesfull".

Some members of that band decided that we wanted to play our own songs. Carles Lloveras, Xavi Palou, Ramon Valls and I created the Maijalas band. The idea was to mix rock with more latin rithms (nowadays I know that is very common). Maijalas got to record a demo tape and a CD. We broke up when we were preparing our second CD. What I listen to

I really like most kinds of music if it is of enough quality. I think there is a time for everything, so these are my favourite bands:


  • When I need "energy": Violent Femmes, The Smiths, David Byrne, Goran Bregovic, Bad Religion, Jane's Addiction, RHCP, Plastilina Mosh, David Bowie or Orchestre Nationale des Barbés
  • When I feel more "latin": Ruben Blades, Compay Segundo (or any other from the Buenavista Social Club), Michel Camilo (or any other from the film Calle 54)
  • When I want some "flamenco": Camarón, Enrique Morente, Pata Negra, Ketama (only the old albums), Kiko Veneno, Martires del Compás, Radio Tarifa
  • Something more "melancholic": Van Morrison, Tom Waits, Neil Young, Thindersticks or Jay Jay Johanson.
  • Some other very good spanish or catalan music: Javier Ruibal, Dusminguet, Radio Futura, Umpah-pah and La Cabra Mecánica.

Latest records I would recommend: I finally decided to listen to Bebo and Cigala's Lagrimas Negras (I usually don't like to give a chance to superhits) and I must admit that it is a great album. I think latest release by Bjork, Medussa, is absolutely awesome. I also like a lot Wilko's A Ghost is Born. I discovered and liked a lot another spanish band: Antònia Font. I also liked latest releases by Fatboy Slim and Enrique Bunbury. Finally I must admit I like Marilyn Manson's covers in Lest We Forget.

Previous recommendations (1): It has been such a long time since the last update that I have many many recommendations to do. I will try to be very brief. My favourites: Kevin Johansen's Sur o no Sur, Josele Santiago's Las Golondrinas, Mala Rodriguez' Alevosia, PJ Harvey's Uh Uh Her, Prince's Musicology, The Street's A Grant Dont Come for Free. I also discovered some new artists that I have ended up liking quite a lot such as Fito y los Fitipaldis, The White Stripes, Tory Amos, Ryan Adams, Franz Ferdinand and especially Adam Green.

Previous recommendations (2): Relax by Los Piratas (excellent), Party Songs by Señor Coconut (funny as hell latin versions of classics like Smoke on The Water), Strays by Jane's Addiction (yeah, I know it is not Ritual de lo Habitual but it has great songs), Actos Inexplicables by Nacho Vegas (extremely surprising), and Sleeping With Ghosts by Placebo (I like those guitars). Some more polemical recommendations (because they are top-selling albums) are The Eminem Show by Eminem (really, I had never heard any lyrics to be so crude, realistic and straight: I don't know of any artist that would dare write what is in 'Cleaning Out My Closet', for instance), and Jarabe de Palo has an excellent song, 'Cambia la piel', in an awful album.

Previous recommendations(3): Flamingos by Enrique Bunbury and The Moldy Peaches' Greatest Hits.
