A Brief Personal Biography

I met my wife Natalia when I was 18. Three years later we rented a flat and went to live together. We got married in 1997 and we spent our honeymoon in Venezuela. We have a son, Aitor, and a daughter, Adriana. Aitor was born on a very particular date 9-9-99. Adriana was born on April 22nd 2003 so she is now about 4 months old.

Now we live in La Garriga, a very nice village 40km from Barcelona. It takes me about an hour to get to my place of work in Barcelona on train.

I was born in 1973 in Barcelona. When I was 5, my parents moved to Santa Eulalia de Ronçana, a littele village 30km from Barcelona where I spent all my childhood. When I was 6 my father died so after that I lived with my mother (Esther) and my sister (Estíbaliz).

When I was 11 I went to the US to study for a year. I stayed with my uncle, aunt and cousins who live in Twin Falls, Idaho. When I came back I had almost forgot all my Spanish and Catalan. But I was also able to get some official English language titles (like the Profficiency level from the University of Cambridge) and start teaching english from a very young age (the first english class I gave was when I was 13). A few years after I started to give some maths and physics private lessons to students who had difficulties with those subjects.

I studied secondary grade (formerly called BUP) in La Vall del Tenes highschool. I changed to Granollers for my last year before university when I learned that some teachers were getting a bit tired of my attittude (so was I). (As a matter of fact the head of my former school tried to talk me out of studying the career I had chosen at University saying that was too difficult for someone who liked partying so much).

During that period of time I also started to play music (see the " My Music" section).. I then went on to study Telecommunications Enginneering to the UPC University in Barcelona. Ever since I have been going to Barcelona on train every day.

Like many young people of that age, during my university studies I was not very much concentrated on studying. I had "more interesting" things to do at that time like playing in a rock band, playing cards in the university bar or reading Joyce's Ulysses.

Even so, I was able to finnish my studies more or less in time (it took me an extra year to finnish a couple of subjects) and I started to work even before I finnished the career.
