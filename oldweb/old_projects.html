Old Projects

What follows is an incomplete list of projects that I was doing at the Music Technology Group at the Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, SPAIN, before I moved to Santa Barbara.

First,I was able to finish my Phd. I enrrolled in the "Computer Science and Digital Communication" PhD organized by the IUA and the Technology Department of the UPF. Please follow visit my PhD page if you would like to learn more about it.

I was (and sort of still am) project manager of a quite ambitious project that is being carried out at the MTG. We are designing and implementing a cross-platform framework for developing music and audio applications and related research. After long discussions we decided the project should be named CLAM (the word has an attractive combination of meanings in English and Catalan). CLAM is implemented in C++ and has already been used for a number of interesting applications. But the best you can do if you happen to be interested on the project is visit its webpage.CLAM is related to the AGNULA European project, focused on the release of a Debian and RedHat distribution to promote the use of Free music and audio applications.

On the other hand, I wasm involved in two other projects: SIMAC and S2S² .
SIMAC is a project about semantic interaction with audio content. One of the outputs of the project are three prototypes: a tool for annotating audio semi-automatically, a music collection organizer and a recommender. I was in charge of leading the developing team.

Finally, I was involved in promoting Free Software. Please visit my page on Free Software and Education.
